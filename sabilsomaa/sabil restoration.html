Restoration Techniques

Based on the documented conditions of the building, it can be affirmed that the current state does not necessitate any major structural interventions or specialized engineering techniques, as the building remains structurally stable. Nevertheless, the monument requires a number of precise conservation interventions, summarized as follows:

  1. Façade Cleaning
    Removal of accumulated dust and surface deposits on the two main façades through appropriate mechanical cleaning methods, including the use of soft brushes, air suction, and low-pressure air blowing techniques, in accordance with conservation standards.
  2. Treatment of Black Stains
    Application of localized poultice cleaning on areas affected by black stains, employing conservation-grade materials and procedures, and strictly adhering to the principles of scientific restoration.
  3. Mitigation of Human-Induced Deterioration
    No direct conservation techniques are required for this type of deterioration. Instead, the proposed measures focus on awareness-raising among users and visitors to enhance their role in safeguarding the building.

The allocation of specific times and designated areas for waste collection is recommended, alongside the involvement of environmental authorities to ensure the prevention of recurring damage.

In conclusion, the building requires only careful preventive conservation measures to safeguard its current structural and architectural integrity, without resorting to further structural reinforcement. Such an approach not only secures the preservation of the monument but also contributes to achieving the objectives of sustainable development.
