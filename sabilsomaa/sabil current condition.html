Current Condition of Sabil Ismail Maghlawi 17/5/2025

Structural Condition

  • The Sabil is currently in a generally good state, almost excellent.
  • It shows no visible cracks or major structural problems that could endanger its stability.

Manifestations of Human-Induced Deterioration

  • The Sabil is subject to deterioration caused by human activities, most notably the repeated disposal of garbage in front of it.
  • It has remained closed for a long period without reuse or rehabilitation following the last restoration, which has negatively affected its overall condition.

Aesthetic Condition

  • Dust has accumulated on the two main façades, diminishing part of the building’s aesthetic value.
  • Black stains have appeared on the stone courses of the façade due to the repeated pouring of water at its base, which, over time, may weaken the mechanical properties of the stones.

Historical Discrepancy

  • Historical records indicate that the Sabil originally consisted of two stories and included two distribution windows.
  • In contrast, its current condition reveals only one story with a main façade containing a single distribution window.

Possible Explanations for the Discrepancy

  • The Sabil may have suffered partial collapse during a past earthquake, leading to the destruction of the upper story and the later relocation of one of the distribution windows.
  • There may have been an error in historical documentation, particularly since another Sabil exists directly opposite the Sabil of Isma‘il al-Maghlouli, sharing nearly identical features, which could have led to confusion between the two during the recording process.
