Mosque of Ibn Barad Bak ( Umm al-Ghulam )

Monument Name: Mosque of Ibn Barad Bak ( Umm al-Ghulam )
Monument Number: 25
Construction Date: (865 AH) - (1460 CE)
Location: Umm Al-Ghulam Street
Founder: Prince Muhammad ibn Barad Bak al-Ashrafi al-Dawadar
Structural Condition: The mosque, in its current state, is closed due to several structural reasons, represented by the large cracks located beneath the minaret at the main facade near the entrance. These cracks are due to either the excessive load on the structure, the weakness of the building materials caused by weather conditions, or the subsidence of the soil level, which has led to these serious structural cracks that must be urgently addressed and repaired. Additionally, factors have contributed to the internal damage of the structure, resulting in the mosque's closure for more than twenty years.
Architectural Description: The external architecture of this monument consists of a single main facade on the southeastern side, featuring a primary entrance with a recessed stone structure covered by a semi-dome supported by four tiers of muqarnas (stalactite-like decorations). Surrounding it is a rectangular frame that converges into a circular motif at its apex. Below this recessed stone, there are two stone niches topped by a recessed band devoid of inscriptions. Between these two niches is a doorway with double wooden panels, above which is a straight lintel made of interlocked stone blocks, followed by a relieving arch also made of interlocked stone blocks. Above this is a recessed area with a muqarnas-decorated chest consisting of two tiers, centered by a rectangular window topped by a smaller rectangular window. On either side of these windows is a prominent Kufic inscription reading, "There is no god but Allah, Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah." Flanking this main entrance are four symmetrical arched niches with muqarnas-decorated chests of two tiers, two on the right and two on the left, with a circular clerestory window between them. Below each of these niches is a window opening covered with a metal lattice screen, topped by a straight lintel of interlocked stone blocks, followed by a relieving arch of interlocked stone blocks as well. Above each niche is a rectangular window with a pointed arch, covered by a fine wire mesh.
